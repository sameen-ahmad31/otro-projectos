University Application, Course Registration, and Advising System

An innovative and comprehensive university management system was created using Python in conjunction with the versatile Flask framework. This powerful combination was further strengthened by the integration of a robust MySQL Database, ensuring the secure storage and retrieval of data, while the utilization of the Amazon RDS Database service contributed to its scalability and high-performance capabilities. The resulting system represents a model of technological integration aimed at revolutionizing the way universities handle their operations.

One of the features of this advanced system is its University Application component. This component empowers prospective students to seamlessly apply to various programs offered by the university. Through a user-friendly interface, applicants can provide their information, upload essential documents, and track their application status, thereby simplifying a once-complex process. Navigating the complex landscape of academic advising is made notably smoother through the system's Advising functionality. Students can view their advisors and access relevant academic information. Beyond benefiting students, the university management system profoundly enhances administrative efficiency. Faculty members can conveniently manage their course materials, grades, and communicate with students, all within a unified platform.

In conclusion, the use of Python, Flask, MySQL, and Amazon RDS has given rise to a university management system that outperforms traditional paradigms. A Data-Driven Exploration of Employment Disparities in Technical Careers and College Majors

During my internship at Virginia Tech - Qualcomm Lab, I worked on a project that analyzed publicly available data to measure employment gaps in various technical (STEM) careers and college majors by ethnicity, sex, and socio-economic status. The focal point of this project was to gather valuable insights from the Institute of Education Sciences: National Center for Education Statistics, a branch of the U.S. Department of Education. By extracting data, I meticulously segregated it into distinct categories for effective analysis. These categories encompassed a wide range of dimensions, including US Higher Education Data, International Data, Educational Attainment/Degrees, Majors, Historically Black Colleges and Universities, and Employment Rates.

To streamline the examination process, I took the additional step of breaking down the data into specific fields, carefully curating them to align with key STEM disciplines. These disciplines encompassed health and medical sciences, environmental studies, biological and life sciences, computer science and data science, engineering, and physical sciences. Each of these fields represented a crucial aspect of the overarching investigation. Within each category, I identified three pivotal variables: ethnicity/race, sex, and socio-economic status. By thoroughly examining these variables, I sought to gain a comprehensive understanding of the disparities and gaps present within various technical careers and college majors. This extensive data curation process was essential in ensuring accurate and relevant results. To facilitate user interaction with the curated data, I developed a sophisticated program that allowed individuals to select and customize their preferences. The output of this program provided users with precisely tailored results, aligning with their specific inputs. Ultimately, this project aimed to shed light on employment gaps in STEM fields, offering valuable insights to inform policy decisions and drive positive change.
